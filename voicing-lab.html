Walking the chords with inversions

A jazz pianist rarely plays chords in root position. They pick the inversion of each chord that keeps common tones held and moves the other voices the shortest distance — so the hand stays put. Compare block (everything in root position) with smooth voice leading and watch the lines go flat.

Read the graph: height = pitch, each column = one chord. In Smooth mode the connecting lines run nearly flat — that's your hand staying in one position while a note or two shifts by a half or whole step. Flip to Block and the same progression turns into staircases of leaps. The inversion label under each chord (Root / 1st / 2nd / 3rd) tells you which chord tone landed in the bass.
