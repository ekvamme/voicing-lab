SONGS WE CAN ADD TO VOICING LAB
================================
All compositions public domain in the US (published 1930 or earlier).
Sourcing rule: machine-readable transcriptions only (ABC / MusicXML) — no
reading from scans. Check abcnotation.com first; trad-jazz tunes are usually
there. Details & legal notes in PUBLIC_DOMAIN_STANDARDS.md.

DONE
  Sweet Georgia Brown (1925) — from Nottingham Music Database ABC

BEST NEXT PICKS (great teaching value, likely in ABC collections)
  St. Louis Blues (1914, W.C. Handy) — blues + habanera section, trad staple
  Tea for Two (1924, Youmans) — the classic ii-V oscillation
  Bye Bye Blackbird (1926) — beginner-friendly AABA
  After You've Gone (1918) — early-jazz iv-I motion
  (Back Home Again in) Indiana (1917) — parent tune of "Donna Lee"
  Avalon (1920) — cycle-of-dominants like SGB
  Whispering (1920) — parent tune of "Groovin' High"
  Tiger Rag (1917) — trad session warhorse
  Ja-Da (1918) — simple, fun, very likely in ABC
  Royal Garden Blues (1919) — trad blues standard

ENGINE NOTES FOR NEW TUNES
  - dim7 quality needed for: Georgia on My Mind, and most 1920s tunes' passing
    chords if we want their full harmony (4 notes; small QUAL table addition)
  - Tunes with pickup-bar melodies (e.g. St. Louis Blues) may need an anacrusis
    field in the melody model
  - Verse vs. chorus: encode the CHORUS only (that's the jazz-standard part)
